## Understanding your options: what kind of website do you need?

### Business/corporate website

**What it is:** A professional website that presents your business — services, about, team, portfolio, contact information.

**Who needs it:** Service businesses, professionals (CAs, doctors, lawyers), consultancies, agencies, manufacturers.

**Typical pages:** Homepage, Services, About, Portfolio/Projects, Testimonials, Contact, Blog.

**Budget range:** ₹25,000 – ₹1,50,000

### E-commerce website

**What it is:** An online store where customers can browse products, add to cart, and purchase.

**Who needs it:** Retailers, D2C brands, artisans, food businesses wanting online ordering.

**Key features:** Product catalogue, cart, checkout, payment gateway (Razorpay, PayU), inventory management, order tracking.

**Budget range:** ₹80,000 – ₹5,00,000

### Landing page / single-page website

**What it is:** A focused, single-page website designed for one specific purpose — usually lead generation.

**Who needs it:** Businesses running ads, launching a new product, or testing a market.

**Budget range:** ₹10,000 – ₹40,000

### Web application

**What it is:** A complex, interactive platform with user accounts, dashboards, data processing, or custom workflows.

**Who needs it:** SaaS companies, educational platforms, booking systems, custom business tools.

**Budget range:** ₹2,00,000 – ₹20,00,000+

## Technology choices: WordPress vs modern frameworks

This is one of the most important decisions in your web development project, and most business owners don't even know they have a choice.

### WordPress

**What it is:** A content management system (CMS) that powers about 40% of all websites globally. Uses templates and plugins for functionality.

**Pros:**
- Huge ecosystem of themes and plugins
- Easy content editing for non-technical users
- Most developers know WordPress
- Large community and support resources

**Cons:**
- Performance issues — WordPress sites with multiple plugins often load slowly
- Security vulnerabilities — the most targeted CMS by hackers due to its popularity
- Plugin dependency — every feature needs a plugin, and plugins break, conflict, and need updates
- Limited customisation without heavy development

**Best for:** Content-heavy websites (blogs, news sites), businesses that need frequent content updates without developer involvement, projects with very tight budgets.

### Next.js / React

**What it is:** Modern JavaScript frameworks that build fast, SEO-optimised websites with a component-based architecture.

**Pros:**
- Significantly faster page loads (server-side rendering)
- Built-in SEO optimisation
- Better security (no plugin vulnerabilities)
- Highly customisable without limitations
- Better mobile experience
- Future-proof technology

**Cons:**
- Requires skilled developers (fewer available than WordPress developers)
- Higher initial development cost
- Content editing requires a CMS integration or developer involvement

**Best for:** Businesses that prioritise speed, SEO, and user experience. Service businesses, agencies, startups, and any business using their website as a primary lead generation tool.

### Our recommendation

For most Jamshedpur businesses in 2025, a Next.js website offers significantly better performance and SEO — which directly translates to more Google traffic and more leads. The higher initial cost is recovered quickly through better organic rankings and conversion rates.

WordPress remains a valid choice for very content-heavy sites or extremely tight budgets, but only if set up correctly with minimal plugins and proper caching.

## The web development process (what to expect)

A professional web development project follows these phases:

### Phase 1: Discovery (Week 1)
- Understanding your business, customers, and goals
- Competitive analysis — what are your competitors doing online?
- Defining the scope — pages, features, functionality
- Written proposal with clear scope, timeline, and cost

### Phase 2: Design (Weeks 2–3)
- Wireframes — structural layouts showing content placement
- Visual design — applying your brand to the wireframes
- Design review and revisions (typically 2–3 rounds)
- Design approval before development begins

### Phase 3: Development (Weeks 3–5)
- Building the website based on approved designs
- Integrating content (text, images, videos)
- Setting up contact forms, WhatsApp integration, analytics
- Mobile optimisation and cross-browser testing

### Phase 4: Testing and Launch (Week 5–6)
- QA testing across devices and browsers
- Speed optimisation and performance tuning
- SEO setup — title tags, meta descriptions, sitemap, Search Console
- Content review and final changes
- Launch and DNS configuration

### Phase 5: Post-Launch (Ongoing)
- Training on content management
- Bug fixes and minor adjustments
- Ongoing maintenance and updates

**Total timeline:** 4–8 weeks for a standard business website. E-commerce and web applications take 8–16 weeks.

## How to choose a developer in Jamshedpur

### Check their portfolio (live sites only)

Don't accept screenshots or design mockups. Ask for 3–5 live website links. Open them on your phone. Check:
- Does it load fast? (Under 3 seconds)
- Does it look modern and professional?
- Is it easy to navigate on mobile?
- Are the designs unique or clearly template-based?

### Verify their technical expertise

Ask what technology they use and why. A developer who can explain the tradeoffs between WordPress and modern frameworks understands their craft. One who says "we use WordPress for everything" may be limited.

### Review their process

A professional developer has a structured process — discovery, design, development, testing, launch. If someone says "just send me your content and I'll have it done next week," they're building a template site, not a custom solution.

### Check references

Ask for client references and actually contact them. One conversation with a past client tells you more than any portfolio.

### Evaluate communication

During your initial discussions, assess how they communicate. Do they respond promptly? Do they ask good questions? Do they explain things clearly? This is a preview of what the actual project experience will be like.

## Common mistakes Jamshedpur businesses make

**Choosing on price alone.** The cheapest quote almost always means the worst website. A ₹10,000 website that doesn't rank, doesn't convert, and breaks within a year costs you far more in lost business than a ₹50,000 website that works.

**Not defining scope clearly.** "Build me a website" is not a scope. How many pages? What features? How many revision rounds? What content will you provide vs what the developer creates? Get everything in writing.

**Ignoring mobile.** Your website will be viewed on phones 75–90% of the time. If the developer shows you desktop designs first and treats mobile as an afterthought, that's a problem.

**Skipping SEO.** A beautiful website that Google can't find is useless. SEO should be built into the development process, not bolted on afterwards.

**No maintenance plan.** Websites need ongoing updates, security patches, and content changes. Plan for ₹5,000–₹15,000/year in maintenance costs.
