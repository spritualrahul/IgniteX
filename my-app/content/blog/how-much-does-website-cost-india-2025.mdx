One of the first questions every business owner asks when they decide to get a website built is: how much is this going to cost me?

The honest answer is — it depends. But that's not helpful. So let's break it down properly, with real numbers, real expectations, and no fluff.

This guide covers what websites actually cost in India in 2025, what you get at each price point, and how to make sure you don't overpay or underpay for what your business actually needs.

## Why website pricing in India varies so much

You can get a website built for ₹5,000. You can also pay ₹5,00,000 for one. Both are real quotes you'll receive from real agencies and freelancers in India.

The gap exists because "website" means very different things. A one-page portfolio site built on a free template is technically a website. So is a custom-built e-commerce platform handling 10,000 orders a day. The word covers an enormous range.

The price depends on four main factors:

- **Complexity** — how many pages, features, and custom functionality you need
- **Design** — template-based vs fully custom design
- **Technology** — WordPress vs custom code vs full-stack web app
- **Who builds it** — fresher freelancer vs experienced agency vs premium studio

Let's go through each price tier honestly.

## ₹5,000 – ₹15,000 — The bare minimum

At this price point, you're getting a website built by a student, a fresher freelancer, or someone using a drag-and-drop builder like Wix or a free WordPress theme.

**What you typically get:**
- 3–5 pages (Home, About, Services, Contact)
- A free or very cheap template, slightly customised
- Basic contact form
- Mobile responsive (usually)
- No SEO setup, no performance optimisation

**Who it's right for:** Someone who just needs a basic online presence and has almost no budget. A visiting card website.

**The risks:** These sites often have poor performance, no ongoing support, and templates that dozens of other businesses are using. If the freelancer disappears after delivery — which happens often at this price — you're on your own.

**Verdict:** Acceptable as a very temporary placeholder. Not suitable for a business that wants to grow online.

## ₹15,000 – ₹50,000 — The small business tier

This is where most small business websites in India get built. At this range you can expect a proper freelancer or a small agency with some experience.

**What you typically get:**
- 5–10 pages with proper content sections
- A premium WordPress theme (Astra, GeneratePress, Divi) or a custom design
- Basic on-page SEO setup (meta titles, descriptions, sitemap)
- Google Analytics and Search Console integration
- Mobile optimised and reasonably fast
- 1–3 months of basic support

**Who it's right for:** Restaurants, coaching centres, local service businesses, professionals (CAs, doctors, lawyers), small retailers.

**The risks:** Quality varies enormously in this range. A ₹20,000 website from a skilled freelancer can be excellent. The same budget with the wrong person gives you a bloated WordPress site with 30 plugins that loads in 8 seconds.

**Verdict:** The right range for most small businesses, provided you vet the developer properly. Ask for past work, check site speed on their portfolio links, and get a clear scope in writing.

## ₹50,000 – ₹1,50,000 — The professional tier

This is where you start getting proper agency-level work. Custom design, clean code, real SEO, and a team that knows what they're doing.

**What you typically get:**
- Fully custom UI/UX design (not a template)
- Built on modern technology — Next.js, React, or a well-architected WordPress
- Page speed score above 80 on mobile
- Full on-page SEO setup with keyword research
- CMS integration so you can update content yourself
- Proper testing across devices and browsers
- 3–6 months of support and maintenance
- Sometimes includes a basic brand identity (logo, colour palette)

**Who it's right for:** Growing businesses, startups, companies that use their website as a primary sales or lead generation tool, businesses running Google Ads (a slow or poor website wastes your ad budget).

**Verdict:** This is the sweet spot for serious businesses. You're paying for craft, not just output. The website will actually help you rank, convert, and grow.

## ₹1,50,000 – ₹5,00,000 — The advanced tier

At this range you're building something with real complexity — custom features, integrations, larger content architecture, or a proper web application.

**What you typically get:**
- Complex functionality — booking systems, portals, dashboards, API integrations
- Custom backend development
- E-commerce with payment gateway, inventory, order management
- Multi-language or multi-location support
- Advanced SEO strategy with content planning
- Performance engineering for high traffic
- Dedicated project manager and design team
- Ongoing retainer for maintenance and growth

**Who it's right for:** E-commerce businesses, SaaS products, educational platforms, healthcare portals, businesses with specific workflow automation needs.

**Verdict:** Justified when the website is a core business tool, not just a brochure. The ROI at this level comes from what the site does, not just how it looks.

## ₹5,00,000+ — Enterprise and product tier

This is full product development territory. Custom web applications, enterprise platforms, large-scale e-commerce, or complex SaaS products.

Most small and medium businesses in India do not need this. If you're reading this article wondering what a website costs, this tier isn't relevant for you yet.

## The hidden costs most people forget

Whatever tier you're in, these additional costs catch people off guard:

**Domain name:** ₹800 – ₹1,500 per year for a .com. ₹500 – ₹800 for .in.

**Hosting:** ₹2,000 – ₹15,000 per year depending on quality. Don't go with the cheapest option — bad hosting = slow site = bad SEO.

**SSL certificate:** Usually included free with good hosting (Let's Encrypt). If someone charges separately for SSL in 2025, that's a red flag.

**Email hosting:** A professional email like contact@yourbusiness.com costs ₹1,500 – ₹6,000/year (Google Workspace, Zoho Mail).

**Annual maintenance:** A good developer will charge ₹5,000 – ₹20,000/year for updates, security patches, and minor changes. Budget for this — websites need maintenance.

**Content:** Writing, photos, and videos are often not included in website quotes. If you need the agency to create content too, that adds ₹10,000 – ₹50,000+ depending on volume.

## How to avoid getting ripped off

A few rules when hiring a web developer or agency in India:

**Always ask for a portfolio with live links.** Open those links on your phone, check how fast they load. A developer's portfolio is the best predictor of what your site will look like.

**Get everything in writing.** Scope, timeline, number of revisions, what's included and what's not. Verbal agreements fall apart.

**Don't pay 100% upfront.** A standard split is 50% to start, 50% on delivery. Never pay everything before seeing anything.

**Ask who actually does the work.** Some agencies outsource to the cheapest freelancer they can find. Ask directly: who on your team will build my site?

**Cheap is expensive in the long run.** A ₹8,000 website that loads in 9 seconds and never ranks on Google costs you far more in lost business than a ₹40,000 website that actually works.
